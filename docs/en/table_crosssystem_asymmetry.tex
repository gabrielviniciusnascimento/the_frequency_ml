\begin{tablenotes}[flushleft]\footnotesize
\item[a] Null-model sample (ages 20--69, both hands valid). The clustering pipeline used
$N{=}8{,}304$ (complete 6-trial cases).
\item[b] MGXH1/H2 encode test \emph{order}, not anatomical side; reconstructed by crossing
with MGATHAND (1$=$right, 2$=$left tested first). 1{,}128 rows excluded for missing
MGATHAND --- no side was imputed.
\item[c] The auditory and grip extreme cases each passed an individual sanity check (raw
trials, special-code/effort flags, plausibility); the vision extremes were not audited
row-by-row.
\item[d] \textbf{Artifact, not structure.} The bilateral vision space has only two features,
so PCA collapses to one component; the high silhouette and the 38 HDBSCAN micro-clusters
reflect a strongly peaked 1-D distribution plus the 0.25\,D measurement grid, not discrete
subtypes. These two rows are not comparable to the auditory/grip values.
\item[e] Each copula is regenerated $B{=}2{,}000$ times (\texttt{RandomState($b$)}); we report
the real count against the null-count mean, with empirical $p=(\#\{\text{null}\ge\text{real}\}{+}1)/(B{+}1)$
(floor $5{\times}10^{-4}$). This supersedes the single-realization ``X\,:\,0'' counts of
earlier drafts. Source: \texttt{audit\_01\_mc\_envelope}.
\item[f] Below the Bonferroni threshold $0.01/12$ (3 systems $\times$ 4 levels). The auditory
excess also survives full unfiltered inclusion ($|z|{>}4$ real/null $\approx$19, $p{=}0.001$;
\texttt{audit\_02}) and a heteroscedastic measurement null at $|z|{\ge}3$ ($p{=}5{\times}10^{-4}$;
\texttt{audit\_04}); the moderate $|z|{>}2$ shoulder does not survive the latter.
\item[g] The contrast tail is bilaterally symmetric: of 69 cases $|R{-}L|{>}50$\,dB, 38 are
right-worse / 31 left-worse (binomial $p{=}0.47$); the often-cited $N{=}13$ group is one
side of it, not a lateralized subgroup (\texttt{audit\_03}).
\item[h] Vision is within/below its null only in the \emph{general} population; under
abnormality-matched inclusion ($\ge$1 eye $|\mathrm{SE}|{\ge}1$\,D) it develops a real excess
($|z|{>}4$ ratio $\approx$5, $p{=}0.001$; \texttt{audit\_02}). Cross-system overlap is
uninformative: grip (2011--2014) and vision (1999--2002) cycles do not intersect.
\item[i] The auditory excess survives the Gaussian, heteroscedastic-measurement, and
tail-dependent Student-t ($\nu{=}4$) nulls (\texttt{audit\_04}, \texttt{audit\_05}); the
grip far-tail survives the Gaussian null but \emph{not} the t-copula ($|z|{>}4$: 35 real vs
null mean 49, $p{=}0.99$), so grip's asymmetry is consistent with bilateral tail dependence,
not a genuine excess. The symmetric contrast tail replicates in OHHR (N$=$574; \texttt{audit\_07}).
\end{tablenotes}
