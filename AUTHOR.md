# Author

**Gabriel Vinícius Nascimento** — independent researcher, Brazil.

I am self-taught and unaffiliated. I build this work — the analysis, the audit, and
the tool — alone and in the open, so it can be checked line by line.

## Why I am the right person for this

I am a **hepatoblastoma survivor** (diagnosed in early childhood, with a late
metastasis years later and long clinical follow-up). Among the lasting effects, the
one that shaped this project is **cisplatin-induced ototoxicity** — and a clinically
precise detail matters here: in my case it affected the **auditory nerve
(retrocochlear/neural)**, not the typical cochlear, outer-hair-cell pattern most
people picture.

That precision is also a limit I state plainly: a threshold-filter simulator (like the
empathy tool in this repo) models **cochlear audibility loss**. It does **not**
represent neural loss — the temporal processing, distortion, and speech-in-noise
difficulty that define *my* hearing. I know the difference, and I will not let the
tool pretend to be my own audiogram. Lived experience is my authority and my
motivation; it is not the argument. The work has to stand on its own.

## How I work

This project is a **methodological critique driven by lived experience**, not a
discovery. I hold every claim to what survives scrutiny — null calibration, multiple
method families, external replication, a contract test against drift — and I downgrade
or withdraw claims that don't. I have already done that publicly: I retracted my own
earlier "lateralized right-ear cluster" headline after auditing my own pipeline, and
reframed the result to the honest one (a continuum of audiogram shapes, plus a real
but bilaterally-symmetric inter-ear contrast that binaural averaging erases).

I am a skeptic by temperament: for every claim there is a counter-claim, and the job
is to defend the *plausible* one at the right strength — falliblly, but usefully — not
to chase certainty and not to inflate.
