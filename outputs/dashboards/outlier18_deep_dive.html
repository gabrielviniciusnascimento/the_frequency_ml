What this means: The 18 are the "wider" version of the 12. Same right-ear severity, but the left ear shows some involvement. This suggests unilateral asymmetry is a continuum — from "completely unilateral" (Cluster 1) to "predominantly unilateral with mild bilateral" (Sub-group 18).

For The Frequency: simulating "unilateral asymmetry" isn't binary. It needs a sliding control: how much each ear is affected.

• All 18 show severe right-ear loss (70–110 dB) with left ear better preserved (0–60 dB)
• Age range: 24–68 — NOT age-related
• 11 males, 7 females — slight male predominance
• Left ear involvement varies: some near-normal (like Cluster 1), others with mild-moderate loss
• This is the "boundary" between the Cluster 1 phenotype and the general population
• Together with Cluster 1: 30 people with unilateral asymmetry across 4+ NHANES cycles
