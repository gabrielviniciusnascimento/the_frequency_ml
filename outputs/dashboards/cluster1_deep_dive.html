How to read the audiograms: Each card shows one person. The red bars are the right ear thresholds, blue bars are the left ear. Higher bars = worse hearing at that frequency. Normal hearing is below 25 dB (the bars would be very short). Notice how the right ear bars are tall (severe loss) while the left ear bars are short (near-normal).

Metadata decoded: Race/ethnicity codes from NHANES: 1=Mexican American, 2=Other Hispanic, 3=Non-Hispanic White, 4=Non-Hispanic Black, 5=Other/Multi. Tinnitus (AUQ191): 1=Yes, 2=No. Hearing difficulty (AUQ054): 1=Excellent, 2=Good, 3=A little trouble, 4=Moderate trouble, 5=A lot of trouble.

Key observations:
• All 12 show the same pattern: severe right-ear loss (65–100 dB) with near-normal left ear (0–40 dB)
• Age range: 20–68 — this is NOT age-related
• 7 males, 5 females — no strong sex bias
• 4 of 8 with available data report tinnitus (50%)
• 0 codes of 666 (no response) — all thresholds are valid measurements, not censoring artifacts
• Appears across 4 different NHANES cycles spanning 15 years — rules out single-cycle equipment bias
• This pattern is clinically recognized: unilateral severe hearing loss warrants investigation for retrocochlear pathology (e.g., vestibular schwannoma)
